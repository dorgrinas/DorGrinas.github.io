E-commerce Business Transaction Analysis - SQL/Oracle

This project focuses on exploring one year of sales data from a UK-based e-commerce store with 500K rows, utilizing SQL for in-depth analysis of transaction trends and customer behaviors. I took the dataset from Kaggle, a platform known for sharing datasets contributed by others.

    The dataset comprises the following columns:

  • TransactionNo (string): Unique six-digit number for each transaction, with "C" indicating cancellations.
  • Date (Date): Date when each transaction was generated.
  • ProductNo (string): Unique five or six-digit character for product identification.
  • Product (string): Name of the product/item.
  • Price (numeric): Price per unit of the product in pound sterling (Â£).
  • Quantity (numeric): Quantity of each product per transaction; negative values indicate cancellations.
  • CustomerNo (string): Unique five-digit number for each customer.
  • Country (string): Name of the country where the customer resides.

Conclusions:

  • Top Products: The top-selling products are diverse, ranging from popcorn holders to kitchen cabinets.
  • Product Categories: Vintage kitchen cabinets have the highest average prices.
  • Cancellation Rate: The cancellation percentage is relatively low at 1.6%.
  • Monthly Sales: Sales peak in November, contributing to a strong holiday season.
  • International Orders: Netherlands and Sweden have the highest average order quantities.
  • Customer Spending: A few customers contribute significantly to total spending.
  • Frequently Purchased Together: Certain product pairs, like Jumbo Bags and Teacups, are frequently purchased together.
